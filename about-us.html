Our Story

Built on a Vision

Marity was founded on the conviction that the most complex challenges humanity faces require a fundamentally new approach — one rooted in the intersection of biology, data science, and engineering.

Our multidisciplinary teams span computational neuroscience, molecular biology, and artificial intelligence. Together, we build systems that mimic, complement, and extend the boundaries of natural intelligence.

We operate as a research-first institution, advancing foundational knowledge while translating discoveries into scalable, real-world solutions. Every product we release is backed by years of rigorous lab validation.

Our Foundation

Core Values

Research Integrity

We uphold the highest standards of scientific rigor. Every claim we make is grounded in reproducible, peer-reviewed methodology.

Radical Collaboration

The most transformative breakthroughs emerge at disciplinary boundaries. We intentionally build teams that span scientific traditions.

Global Impact

Our research is motivated by problems that matter at scale. From disease modelling to climate adaptation, we focus on what shapes the future.

Open Science

We publish our core findings openly, contribute to the global scientific commons, and actively engage with the academic community.

Ethical Design

Technology must be built with accountability in mind. We embed ethics review into every phase of product development and research design.

Biomimicry

Nature has been solving complex problems for billions of years. We extract design principles from biological systems to engineer superior technologies.

Our Journey

Key Milestones

2012

Institution Founded

Marity was established in Oslo, Norway as an independent research center with a founding team of 12 scientists from MIT, ETH Zurich, and Cambridge.

2015

NeuroPilot Protocol Published

Our foundational paper on neural signal translation achieved over 4,200 citations and was featured in Nature Neuroscience.

2018

Glasgow Lab Established

Expanded operations to the United Kingdom with a new molecular biology research facility at Glasgow Science Centre.

2021

BioSynth Engine v1 Released

First commercial AI platform for predictive genetic pathway modelling, adopted by 38 clinical research institutions globally within 18 months.

2026

483+ Technologies Developed

Marity today operates with 175+ completed research projects, a global network of 60+ partner institutions, and a team of 340 scientists and engineers.
